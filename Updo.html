An updo can keep horses' tails clean before showing. This can impress judges, as well as keep the tail healthier. Mud is drying. So, dirty tails get brittle to break.

Here are some tips:

  • Here you see the braider bandaged the tail with a cotton ace. Then, the groom pulled up the length with another bandage. Using two bandages dissipates the pressure. That is less pressure on the hair in the braid, which I recommend.
  • No polo bandages on braids. They slip, which breaks hair. The length maybe be in a polo, but a polo is a very last resort for covering the braid.
  • If your horse wears a fake tail, I would definitely put it up while schooling in the mud. You don't want that heavy wet hair hanging on the dock more than necessary.
  • If the tail were unbraided, I would still put it up until right before showing. Why not look like a million bucks?!
  • Young horses should practice at least once at home. Having the tail up or braided is a different weight. So, let your equine trot around at home to feel and realize it is not a problem. At some point he should settle down and stop swishing/questioning the tail. There are enough new things for green horses at shows. A simple exercise like this can be wise miles under his belt.
  • If tails or legs are muddy ringside, you can get them sparkling clean instantly without drying the hair nor skin. Lucky Braids Whitener/Dry Wash Spray works on ANY COLOR. This enzyme for equines dissolves the bond with dirt, leaving hair super clean and without residue. Dirt and stains wipe off with a towel.

So, set your horse up for success. A little management can keep horses sound of body and mind.
